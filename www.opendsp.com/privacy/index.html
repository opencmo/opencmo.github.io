Platform Privacy Policy

This privacy statement below applies to our OpenDSP online advertising technology platform (the Platform). If you have any questions about this information, please CONTACT US HERE.

THE PLATFORM

OpenDSP is a technology platform (Platform) that allows our clients to buy, through real-time bidding, and serve online advertising, including interest-based, or behavioral, advertising and retargeting, as well as conduct data analysis and run A/B testing experiments. The platform enables clients to make buying decisions using non-PII, which is collected by the clients both utilizing platform~s capabilities and through other channels. Platform uses some of the non-PII information collected by the clients for optimization of clients~ advertising campaigns, for analytics, and reporting.

We receive information about consumers from third parties to enable targeted advertising on the website(s) and application(s) that we support. The collection and use of that data is governed by the privacy policy and legal terms of the data collector and the website using the data; it is not governed by OpenDSP.

OPENDSP COOKIE STATEMENT

Although the specific cookies may change from time to time, this describes the purposes for which the OpenDSP Platform uses cookies. For a detailed description of cookies set by the Platform, see Platform Cookies.

  • The Platform uses unique cookies to identify web browsers and to store data to facilitate clients~ in their advertising, including interest-based advertising; to facilitate clients~ own analytics and modeling; and for Platform~s marketing analytics, predictive analytics, and reporting on behalf of the clients.
  • The Platform uses unique cookies to collect and store data on web browser activities related to clients~ advertising purposes, including data related to ad delivery and reporting data, such as, for example, which ad was shown, the number of times a particular ad has been shown, and how recently an ad has been shown to a particular web browser; as well as responses to the ads, such as clicks, visits to advertiser sites, conversions, etc.;
  • By contract, Platform clients are prohibited from associating Platform cookies with any PII.
  • The Platform uses unique cookies to track users~ responses (clicks and conversions) to ads purchased on the platform.
  • The Platform uses non-unique cookies to store users~ opt-out choices.
  • The Platform enables clients to use information provided by third-party sources. Clients and third parties use ID synching to enable the use of this information through the Platform.
  • The Platform is designed to use only non-PII, and OpenDSP has contractual provisions that prohibit clients of the Platform from associating Platform cookies with PII.

In the context of Non-PII, whenever we refer to ~you~ or ~yours,~ please remember that we may be referring to a computer and not an identifiable person since we generally do not know who is using a computer at any particular time. Data collected by or using the Platform about internet traffic and usage is not personally identifying or identifiable, and is not supplemented or combined with personally identifying or identifiable information by OpenDSP. OpenDSP may share the non-personal, traffic or click-stream information it collects with its advertising clients and certain selected third parties, such as data and service providers, either in raw or in aggregated, but only in anonymous form. OpenDSP~s servers collect non-personal information about the publisher~s website which you visited and may have been presented an offer/advertisement and provides access to that information to the Platform~s clients. For example, an advertising client may have access to the browsing history of anonymous users. Also, OpenDSP notes some of the clients~ websites you visit through the use of pixel tags. The information collected by OpenDSP through the use of these pixel tags is not personally identifiable. In addition to the foregoing, OpenDSP uses the information (in aggregate form) for modeling on behalf of Platform~s clients to optimize anonymous user~s experience with and response to the clients~ advertising.

Furthermore, we collect Non-PII through the use of cookies, pixels and related technologies managed by OpenDSP, our partners and vendors.  A cookie is a file on your browser that uniquely identifies your browser and computer.  A pixel is a line of code which is used by a website or third party to assign online activities to a computer or browser.  The use of a pixel allows websites and us to record, for example, that a user has visited a particular web page.  Additional Non-PII from an earlier period or interaction may be included in the pixel.  Though Non-PII does not personally identify anyone, we are still committed to giving users the opportunity to exercise choice about the Non-PII we collect. 

A cookie is a small text file containing a string of characters that is placed by an online server on your hard drive. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time. We use persistent cookies to store the non-personally identifiable information described above, and to store inferences we make based on that non-personally identifiable information about what a user~s interests might be. Most web browsers are initially set up to accept cookies, but you can change your web browser settings to cause your browser to refuse cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. You can remove and/or disable cookies by following directions provided in your Internet web browser~s Y´help¡ file or menu.

Our cookies do not harm your computer in any way or provide us with any information about your identity. We do not install software of any kind on your computer and have no ability to monitor or track your general computer or overall internet usage. Cookies can be easily avoided by opting out from them or by adjusting your browser settings.

FOR WHAT PURPOSES DO WE USE THE INFORMATION?

  • OpenDSP uses the information collected on the Platform to provide, operate, manage, maintain, and enhance the Platform.
  • OpenDSP may act as a client of the Platform to advertise its business online.
  • The Platform enables OpenDSPF~s clients to use non-PII OpenDSP and clients collect to buy and sell online advertising, including interest-based advertising, using real-time bidding.
  • Particular advertising-related purposes for which OpenDSP~s clients may use the non-PII collected on the platform include:
    • Building models to predict, optimize, and customize clients~ online advertising strategy
    • Interest-based advertising (To opt out of interest-based advertising on the Platform, go to the Y´What are your choices?¡ section below.)
    • Customizing ads to a particular geographic region
    • Running A/B testing experiments to analyze and optimize usersF~ response to the ads served by the clients on the Platform.
    • Reporting aggregated statistics regarding, for example, the effectiveness of online advertising campaigns

Typically, companies using Platform own the data that they provide to and collect through the Platform, while OpenDSP owns and provides access to the data it collects on behalf of its clients. While the Platform is designed to use only non-PII, and Platform clients are prohibited from bringing PII onto the Platform, when clients remove data from the platform, their use of data is governed by their own privacy policies and applicable laws, rules, or regulations.

WHAT INFORMATION DOES OpenDSP SHARE WITH THIRD PARTIES?

OpenDSP may, to operate or enhance Platform~s services, provide access to data collected on the Platform to clients, partners, and service providers. WHAT ARE YOUR CHOICES?

OPT OUT

You can opt out of interest-based advertising.

OpenDSP Web Browser Opt Out: You can click below to opt out of having the Platform used to select ads for your browser based on your online web browsing behavior. When you opt out, an opt-out cookie (from opendsp.com) will be stored in your web browser. The Platform will know the choice you have made when it sees your opt out cookie, and will apply your choice to all companies~ use of the Platform. If you block or delete the opt-out cookie, the Platform will not see your choice.

  • The opt out cookie cannot be set if your browser is configured to block third-party cookies.
  • If you delete your cookies, you will need to opt out again. There are browser plugins to help you preserve your opt out cookies. For more information, please visit HTTP://WWW.ABOUTADS.INFO/PMC.
  • The opt out only applies to the browser in which you set it. For example, if you set the opt out while using Firefox, but then use Chrome, the opt out will not be active in Chrome. To opt out in Chrome, you will need to repeat the opt-out process.
  • The opt out does not block or delete cookies, or prevent the use of cookies or other technologies for purposes other than selecting ads based on your interests as inferred by your online behavior. If you opt out, data may still be collected about your web browsing activities and you will still see advertising. Ads may be selected, for example, based on the content of the web page in which they are shown. If you wish to block or delete cookies altogether, you can use web browser settings to do so. See the RESOURCES ABOVE for more information.
  • To learn more about interest-based advertising, and to access the opt-outs of other online advertising companies, visit the Network Advertising Initiative OPT OUT PAGE.
